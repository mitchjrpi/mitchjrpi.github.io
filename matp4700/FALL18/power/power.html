1 Introduction

ZICO Power operates the power grid for Clark County. They employ multiple methods for generating power: solar arrays, wind farms, hydropower, and a gas-fired steam plant. They have forecasted the wind and solar power available for the next week and wish to decide how to meet the forecasted demand.

All the forecasts are for 6-hour intervals, so power generation decisions are needed for 28 periods. The forecasts for demand, solar availability, and wind power availability can be found on the course webpage and on LMS. At present, ZICO operates a single wind farm and a single solar array. The hydro system consists of four reservoirs, networked together. Water from reservoir A flows into reservoir B, which in turn flows into reservoir C. In addition, water from reservoir D also flows into reservoir C. Solar power can be used to either meet the forecasted demand and/or to pump water uphill from reservoir C to reservoir D. Wind power can be used to either meet the forecasted demand and/or to pump water uphill from reservoir B to reservoir A.

3 Constraints

3.1 The steam plant

The steam plant can generate at most 30MWh in a 6 hour interval. The plant can increase or decrease its production by at most 3MWh from one 6-hour period to the next; this is its maximum ramp-up or ramp-down rate.

3.2 Meet demand

Demand is measured in MWh and has to be met in each 6 hour period. The output of the solar array, the wind farm, and the steam plant are measured in MWh. The total water flowing out of a reservoir and used for power generation in a six-hour period is measured in acre-feet, and has to be scaled to give the power generation in MWh. The conversion factors are as follows:

ReservoirMWh per acre-foot of water
A 1.7
B 1.4
C 1.8
D 1.7

3.3 Pumping water

Some of the power output from the solar array can be used to pump water from Reservoir C to Reservoir D, instead of being used to meet the system-wide demand.

Similarly, some of the power output from the wind farm can be used to pump water from Reservoir B to Reservoir A, instead of being used to meet the system-wide demand.

The number of acre-feet pumped per MWh of output are as follows:

Reservoir Acre-feet of water pumped per MWh of energy
B to A by wind farm 0.4
C to D by solar array 0.5

3.4 Water levels in the reservoirs

Various parameters associated with the reservoirs are given in the next table.

ReservoirInitial water levelCapacityMaximum release rate
A 200 400 15
B 150 350 14
C 400 600 16
D 180 380 17

Initial water level and capacity are measured in acre-feet. The maximum release rate is an upper bound on the amount of acre-feet of water that can be released from a reservoir in a six-hour period.

You may assume water takes 6 hours to flow downstream from one reservoir to the next, and that it is also pumped uphill at the same rate. For example, water that is discharged from Reservoir B at period t arrives in Reservoir C at period t + 1.

You can model the water level in Reservoir j at time t + 1 as equal to its level at time t, minus any outflow at time t (upstream or downstream), plus any incoming rainflow, plus any incoming pumped water that was pumped at time t.

Forecast rainfall over the week is limited:

ReservoirtimeRainfall inflow forecast (acre-feet of water)
A 12 13
D 16 15

No other rainfall is forecast.

3.5 Mitigating end effects

ZICO does not want to use up all its water inventories in a single week, so they impose conservation constraints. In particular, they require that the total energy that could be generated from the water in the reservoirs should be the same at the end of the week as it is at the beginning. For the water in each reservoir, this energy is the sum of the energy available from releasing the water from the current reservoir, plus the energy available from all downstream releases as this water flows through the system.

Additionally, they require that the final water level in each reservoir is at least 80% of its initial level.

They also want to allow the possibility for the steam plant schedule to be repeated from one week to the next. This means that the output from the steam plant in the first period and the last period should differ by no more than 3MWh (the ramp-up rate).

3.6 Spinning reserve

As a contingency against the demand exceeding forecast or generation from wind or solar failing to meet the forecast level, ZICO wishes to ensure that it has enough unused capacity available at any moment in the hydro system and steam plant to generate an additional 10MWh in any six-hour period.

6 AMPL notes

  • If you are working in a Windows operating system, you can edit your model and data files in Office, for example. Save the files as plain text files. Windows may append the suffix txt to the file names, in which case you would need to include that suffix when asking ampl to read the file.
  • If you want to read in a new model file chips.mod and data file chips.dat, you can use the reset command:

    ampl: reset;
    ampl: model chips.mod;
    ampl: data chips.dat;

  • If you want to reset the whole data file and read in a new data file chips.dat, but you want to keep the model file as before, you can type

    ampl: reset data;
    ampl: data chips.dat;
