Case Study:

ZICO Power

Sensitivity Analysis

MATP4700 Math Models of Operations Research

Fall 2018

  1. MobileCharge is interested in placing a mobile charging station at one of the reservoirs for one time period and using some of the power generated by that reservoir. Looking purely at reduced costs, how much do you estimate should ZICO charge MobileCharge per MWh to place a charging station at reservoir D for time period 16? What about reservoir B for time period 2? Do these estimates seem reasonable to you? (You may assume in both cases that the amount of power consumed by MobileCharge is small enough that the set of basic variables is unchanged.)

    Solution:

    The reduced costs for reservoir D at time 16 and reservoir B at time 0 are

    ampl: display hydrorelease, hydrorelease.rc;

    : hydrorelease hydrorelease.rc :=

    B 2 0 -2.13163e-14

    D 16 0 12.9524

    Need to scale by releaseratepower, so cost per MWh for (D,16) is 12.9524∕1.7 = $7.619.

    However, look at the constraint meetdemand:

    subject to meetdemand {t in 1..horizon}:

    sum{i in SOLAR} solarpower[i,t] + sum{j in WIND} windpower[j,t]

    + steamgen[t] + sum{h in HYDRO} releaseratepower[h]*hydrorelease[h,t]

    >= demand[t];

    When we increase hydrorelease, we are implicitly increasing the amount of power available for use in the system. But if we sell the power externally, it’s not available internally. So the true price is to add the numbers found above to the shadow price meetdemand. We have, from question 1,

    ampl: display demand, meetdemand, meetdemand.up, meetdemand.down;

    : demand meetdemand meetdemand.up meetdemand.down :=

    2 95 23.8095 102.16 92.08

    16 40 16.1905 50.5294 35.7059

    Adding these numbers to the earlier numbers gives a price of $23.8095 per MWh in each case.

The model power_hi.mod includes an incentive for external sales of hydropower. You can try different prices for the sale of the MWh generated by one acre-foot of release. The values of the new parameter at which ZICO is indifferent between selling or not are hydroincentive['B',2]=100/3 and hydroincentive['D',16]=850/21. Note that 23.8095 is approximately 500/21.
