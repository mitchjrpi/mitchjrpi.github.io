  • 5.5.16

    (a) The dual problem is on page 179. It is stated there that in the dual problem “we try to minimize the total value to us of the ingredients that we must use by setting their prices yi, while ensuring that the value we place on the ingredients that go into one keg of each variety of beer is at least equal to the revenue we get from selling that keg of product.”

    The dual constraints require that the total price of the ingredients for each variety of beer are at least equal to the price of the beer. The dual variables then give us the value of one unit of each ingredient.

    (b) The primal variables are the shadow prices for the dual. Their optimal values can be found from the optimal reduced costs of the dual slack variables.

    (c) The dual constraints correspond to constraints on prices, so the xj* are the shadow prices for these price constraints. For example, the first dual constraint 7y1 + y2 + 2y3 ≥ 90 corresponds to x1 and so x1 is the shadow price for the first price constraint.
