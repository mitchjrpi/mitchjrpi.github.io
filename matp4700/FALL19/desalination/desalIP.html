1 Forecasting future demand and expanding capacity

Abacus Water has predicted that demand for freshwater will increase over the next 20 years, so they need to construct additional desalination plants. There are three possible locations:

  • Plant P5, connected to city B.
  • Plant P6, connected to city D.
  • Plant P7, connected to city F.

Each additional plant would incur additional operational costs per 1,000m3 of purified water supplied. The new plants would use only renewable energy sources. An improved disposal system would be installed at each of the new plants, so all brine produced at the new plants can be disposed of without additional charges.

They have various daily capacity options at each location. At most one of the options is chosen at each location, so Abacus Water could build up to 3 new desalination plants, one at each location.

In addition to operational costs, Abacus Water would incur construction costs. Each capacity option has a corresponding construction cost. These costs are represented as a daily cost in the data file.

2 Renewable energy

Let P denote the amount of purified water produced by the original four desalination plants. Abacus Water is now allowed to use more non-renewable energy sources, but only if they pay for any carbon used beyond 0.05P. In particular, Abacus Water has obtained the option to purchase carbon offsets. These must be purchased in blocks of size equivalent to the energy required to produce 105m3 of purified water. The cost of such a block is $5,000.

For example, assume Abacus Water supplies P = 5.5 × 105m3 purified water from the original plants, and 1.5 × 105m3 of this was produced using non-renewable energy sources. We have 0.05P = 0.275 × 105m3, so Abacus Water would have to offset 1.225 × 105m3 due to carbon usage. Thus, they would have to buy 2 blocks for a cost of $10,000.

3 Questions

All the data are contained in a modified data file, desalIP.dat. Updated parameter definitions can be found in a modified model file, desalIP.mod. (Note: The given model file will run, but it doesn’t impose any limits or costs on P5, P6, or P7, and it doesn’t allow for carbon offsets.) Versions in rtf format of the model and data files are available; these won't run in ampl, but they should easily show you the changes from the original model and data files.

Add variables and constraints to the model file, and modify the objective function in order to model the new features. Solve the modified integer program.

You can use your own model file if you prefer, provided it accurately solves the original linear optimization problem.

What is the optimal value? (Hint: It should be somewhere between $650,000 and $700,000 per day.) What additional plants are constructed, with which option? How many carbon offset blocks are purchased?
