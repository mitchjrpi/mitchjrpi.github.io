Semidefinite programming and integer programming

The principal areas of application of semidefinite programming are in control theory and integer programming. This is a selection of the many papers on SDP and integer programming. Goemans and Williamson [21] started much of the excitement in this area by presenting an algorithm for MAXCUT that has very good guaranteed performance. Anjos and Wolkowicz [7] and de Klerk et al. [19] look at further improving the relaxations presented in [21]. Zhao et al. [67] and Anstreicher et al. [11] examine the quadratic assignment problem (see also [8,10]). Benson et al. [16] look at a dual method, which has better sparsity properties. Helmberg and Rendl [33,31] described a branch-and-cut method for MAXCUT problems and I [40] looked at improving the performance of this algorithm by using a warm start. Helmberg [30] developed criteria for determining when a variable can be fixed at zero or one.
