Semidefinite programming (SDP) theory

A semidefinite program usually requires minimizing a linear function of a matrix subject to the matrix being positive semidefinite and perhaps some extra constraints. Interior point methods for linear programming can be extended to algorithms for semidefinite programming. Alizadeh et al. discuss convergence rates, stability [2] and nondegeneracy [1] of SDP problems and algorithms. Helmberg et al. [26,27] discuss a bundle method for solving SDP problems, which uses a technique from nonsmoooth optimization applied to a dual formulation. Muramatsu [38,39] shows that affine algorithms may fail to converge to the optimal solution of a SDP problem. Pataki [40] examines properties of the optimal solutions of SDP problems. Ramana et al. [41] show that the primal and dual optimal values for a SDP may not be equal and they discuss conditions to ensure that the values are equal. Todd [46] shows how the many different directions proposed for SDPs are related. Zhang [55] discusses extending proof techniques from the linear programming case to the SDP case.
