Semidefinite programming and integer programming

The principal areas of application of semidefinite programming are in control theory and integer programming. This is a selection of the many papers on SDP and integer programming. Goemans and Williamson [17] started much of the excitement in this area by presenting an algorithm for MAXCUT that has very good guaranteed performance. Anjos and Wolkowicz [6] and de Klerk et al. [15] look at further improving the relaxations presented in [17]. Zhao et al. [57] and Anstreicher and Brixius [7,9] examine the quadratic assignment problem. Benson et al. [13] look at a dual method, which has better sparsity properties. Helmberg and Rendl [28] described a branch-and-cut method for MAXCUT problems and I [32] looked at improving the performance of this algorithm by using a warm start. Helmberg [25] developed criteria for determining when a variable can be fixed at zero or one.
