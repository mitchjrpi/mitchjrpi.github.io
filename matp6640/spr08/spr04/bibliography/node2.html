Papers for your presentations

I maintain a collection of references at

http://www.rpi.edu/~mitchj/optim.bib
where you can find URL's for most of the tech reports and some of the published papers given in the bibliography. A searchable version is also available at:
http://liinwww.ira.uka.de/bibliography/Math/optim.html
The searchable version makes the URL information clickable.

For more background, look at the survey paper by Steve Wright [42] and a paper on the use of homogenization techniques to prove many results about linear programming and interior point methods by Terlaky [36]. Introductory material for semidefinite programming can be found in Alizadeh [2] and in Vandenberghe and Boyd [39].

The following is a list of papers that either discuss some of the topics I covered in more detail or discuss extensions of the material. You can pick a paper from this list to present, or you can suggest a different paper, or I will suggest a paper. In some cases, it will be appropriate to present two papers. I have copies of all of these papers, and you can find many of them either in the library or online.

You will find your name in the list of papers, indicating the paper I would like you to present. If you want to suggest something else, feel free to do so; the alternative doesn't have to be drawn from this list. The order of presentations will be random, and will be determined next week.
