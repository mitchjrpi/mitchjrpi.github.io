Abstract: We describe a branch and cut algorithm for both MAX-SAT and weighted MAX-SAT. This algorithm uses the GSAT procedure as a primal heuristic. At each node we solve a linear programming (LP) relaxation of the problem. Two styles of separating cuts are added: resolution cuts and odd cycle inequalities. We compare our algorithm to the Davis-Putnam algorithm. Our algorithm is more effective than Davis-Putnam on some problems, notably MAX-2-SAT. Davis-Putnam is more effective on some other classes of problems.
